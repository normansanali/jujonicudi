Brittany Murphy Net Worth: Career &amp; Death

The estimated net worth of Brittany Anne Murphy-Monjack, also known as Brittany Murphy, was $10 million at the time of her death.

The actress and singer made her demise because of pneumonia in 2009, not because of an accidental drug overdose, contrary to the popular legend.

She rose to fame in the mid-1990s with roles in movies including “Clueless.”

Prior to making her Broadway stage debut with “A View from the Bridge,” Murphy concentrated more on independent films like “Freeway” and “Bongwater.”

She made a comeback to big-budget movies in the late 1990s with “Girl, Interrupted” and “Drop Dead Gorgeous.”

Brittany Murphy | Net Worth and Income

Brittany Murphy | Real Estate

Brittany spent $3.85 million on the house in Los Angeles, California, in June 2003. She bought the house from pop icon Britney Spears.

Years later, the exact same house was the scene of the deaths of both Brittany and her husband, Simon Monjack.

A few months after Monjack’s passing, Britanny’s mother, Sharon Murphy, tried selling the house for $7.25 million.

She was successful in selling it for $2.7 million in July 2011. The house had a reputation for being “haunted” following its owner’s unexpected passing; thus, selling it was difficult.

Brittany Murphy | Personal Life

After her parents divorced, her mother raised Brittany in a Baptist home in Edison, New Jersey, along with two half-brothers and a half-sister.

In order to advance her profession, Murphy actually asked her mother if they could relocate to Los Angeles, and her mother approved.

For the sake of her daughter to pursue her prospective career, her mother moved the whole family to Los Angeles in 1991.

As for her love life, Brittany’s first high-profile relationship was with actor and model Ashton Kutcher in 2002.

She then dated several other individuals within the entertainment industry.

She was formerly engaged to Jeff Kwatinetz, a talent manager, and then got engaged to Joe Macaluso, a production assistant.

In the end, Murphy wed Simon Monjack, a British screenwriter, in May 2007 in a secret Jewish ceremony. The two then spent their final few years living together in a house in Los Angeles.

According to reports, Brittany passed out on December 20, 2009, in her Los Angeles residence. Firefighters unsuccessfully attempted to resuscitate.

Unfortunately, after being taken to a local hospital, she passed away a short while later.

Cardiac arrest was reported as the cause of death. However, months later, authorities revealed that her primary cause of death was pneumonia.

Brittany Murphy | Charity

The Brittany Murphy Foundation was established in January 2010 by Murphy’s mother Sharon and her widower Simon Monjack to promote children’s arts education, the USO, and cancer research.

In February 2010, the foundation launched a fundraiser event at the Saban Theatre in Beverly Hills, California.

An examination of the foundation’s documents revealed that the foundation’s not-for-profit status had not been filed.

The foundation’s website then briefly went offline before reopening a few days later with a notice announcing that donations would no longer be accepted.

According to a statement published on the foundation’s website, Murphy’s father, Angelo Bertolotti, formally reopened the Brittany Murphy Foundation in November 2013.

No information on the charity has surfaced since the passing of Murphy’s father in January 2019 at the age of 92.

Brittany Murphy | Career

Brittany began receiving professional acting, singing, and dance training in New Jersey when she was four.

Murphy made her television debut in 1991 when she appeared in a “Murphy Brown” episode on CBS.

Soon after, she appeared in a number of new TV programs, including “Blossom,” “Murder One,” and “Frasier.”

Additionally, she was cast in recurring roles on television series like “Boy Meets World” and “Sister, Sister.”

Murphy’s breakthrough role came with “Clueless,” which was released in 1995.

Before obtaining a voice acting part in “King of the Hill,” she followed up with “Drop Dead Gorgeous” after a couple of more small flicks.

Her premature death in 2009 put an end to her career after a few more high-profile movies during the 2000s.
