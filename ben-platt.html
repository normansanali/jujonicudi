Ben Platt - Bio, Career, Age, Net Worth, Height, Nationality, Facts

Ben Platt is a popular American actor, singer, and songwriter. Ben Platt began his acting career in musical theater as a child artist and appeared in productions of The Sound of Music and The Book of Mormon.

Early Life and Childhood

Ben Platt was born on September 24, 1993, in the city of Los Angeles, California, United States. His age is 30 years as of 2023 and his full name is Benjamin Schiff Platt.

Similarly, he is American by nationality and Christian by religion. Moreover, he belongs to the Jewish ethnicity and his zodiac sign is Libra.

He was born to a film, television, and theater producer Marc Platt (father), and a housewife Julie Platt (mother). Likewise, his siblings’ names are Jonah Platt, Henry Platt, and Hannah Platt.

Career and Professional Life

Moving on to his career, he co-starred with Kristin Chenoweth in The Music Man at the Hollywood Bowl at the age of nine. Similarly, at the age of 11, he performed on a national tour of Caroline. Likewise, he played Jean Valjean in Kidz Theater’s production of Les Miserables when he was 17 years old.

Moreover, in 2012 he was cast as Elder Arnold Cunningham in The Book of Mormon’s Chicago production. Furthermore, he appeared as a supporting character in the 2012 musical comedy Pitch Perfect. In 2014, he joined Steven Levenson and Pasek and Paul’s then-untitled P&P Project.

In addition, he made his Broadway debut in the title role of Dear Evan Hansen at the Music Box Theatre in December 2016. Later, he signed a record deal with Atlantic Records in 2017. He also performed Leonard Bernstein’s “Somewhere” live at the 60th Annual Grammy Awards on January 28, 2018.

He announced in January 2019 that his debut studio album, Sing to Me Instead, would be released on March 29, 2019. Similarly, he also revealed on Instagram that the recording of his Radio City concert would be released on Netflix as a TV special titled Ben Platt Live from Radio City Music Hall on May 20, 2020.

Besides that, he teased the chorus of his new single, “Imagine,” on TikTok in April 2021. Furthermore, on June 9, 2022, it was announced that he would produce and star in the musical comedy film Theater Camp, which was inspired by his previous short film of the same name.

Moreover, on August 31, 2022, he confirmed that he was working on new solo music and was a third of the way through finishing his next album.
