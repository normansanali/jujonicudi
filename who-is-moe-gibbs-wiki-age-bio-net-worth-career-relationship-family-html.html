The Shocking Crime

  • Moe Gibbs, a prison officer, faced a grave accusation in 2007 for the murder of Mindy Morgenstern, a 22-year-old student at Valley City State University.
  • The young victim was discovered lifeless in her apartment in September 2006, with evidence pointing towards strangulation and deep neck wounds.

Arrest and DNA Evidence

  • Authorities made the arrest based on DNA evidence found under the victim’s fingernails, which connected Gibbs to the crime scene.
  • Disturbingly, DNA matching Gibbs’s was also linked to an unsolved rape case from two years prior to Morgenstern’s tragic murder.

Trial and Verdict

  • Gibbs’s trial unveiled shocking revelations, including testimonies from ex-girlfriends who exposed his pattern of infidelity.
  • In a harrowing turn of events, Moe Gibbs was found guilty and sentenced to life in prison without the possibility of parole.
  • He is currently incarcerated at the North Dakota State Penitentiary in Bismarck, serving his sentence for the heinous crime.

Family and Relationships

NBC’s In-Depth Coverage

The DNA Mystery Unfolds
