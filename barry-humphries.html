Barry Humphries was a talented Australian comedian, actor, author, and satirist who found huge popularity by dressing up in drag as the characters Dame Edna Everage and Sir Les Patterson. He was cherished for his Dadaist and absurdist humor and was considered to be ‘the most significant comedian to emerge since Charlie Chaplin‘.

    Barry Humphries Facts

  • He was an avid art collector and, at one point, owned the largest private collection of Charles Conder’s artworks in the world.
  • Barry suffered from Asperger’s syndrome.
  • He had worked as a Sunday school teacher in Melbourne for a few years in the 1950s.
  • In 2007, Barry was awarded the C.B.E. (Commander of the Order of the British Empire) at Queen Elizabeth II‘s Birthday Honors List for his services to entertainment.
  • Joan Rivers was one of his closest friends.
  • Barry received an Honorary Doctorate from Griffith University in 1994.
  • If he could have had dinner with any 2 famous people, it would have been the writer Vladimir Nabokov and actress Margot Robbie.
  • Barry had once said that he would like to be remembered for making people forget about their worries and laugh a little.
  • He was a self-proclaimed bibliomaniac and had a collection of nearly 25,000 books, including the rarest and arcane books from the 19th and 20th centuries.
  • Barry was also a talented landscape painter.
