Shastri’s cricket journey boasts remarkable achievements. Commencing as a bowler, he evolved into an all-rounder during his tenure with the Indian team. His leadership as captain propelled India to significant accomplishments, solidifying its status as one of the world’s formidable teams. Post his cricket retirement, Shastri remains actively engaged in the sports realm. Presently, he holds the position of Head Coach for the Indian Cricket Team while concurrently managing his entrepreneurial pursuits.

Coaching and Entrepreneurship

The majority of Shastri’s income comes from his role as the head coach of the Indian Cricket Team, which is managed by the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI). In addition to his coaching duties, he has also ventured into entrepreneurship. In 2003, he started a talent management company called Showdiff Worldwide in collaboration with Rediffusion DY&R.

Beyond his financial success and notable accomplishments, Shastri is recognized for his philanthropic endeavors and advocacy for emerging athletes. He actively participates in diverse initiatives aimed at fostering sports and providing support to aspiring talents.

Luxuries and Assets

When it comes to assets, Shastri lives in a luxurious house in Mumbai, India, with an estimated value of around Rs.11.2 crores. He also owns a collection of luxury cars, including brands like Mercedes Benz, Ford, and BMW. Recently, he purchased a brand-new luxury car worth $100,000.

Achieving Milestones

Throughout his career, Shastri achieved several milestones. He holds the record for the best figures in the Irani Trophy, with figures of 9/101, a record that stood for nearly 20 years. In recognition of his contributions to the sport, he was named the national goodwill ambassador for UNICEF after the legendary Sachin Tendulkar. He also had the honor of being the celebrity torchbearer during the Olympic Torch Relay in Oman in 2008.

The Journey of a Legend

In his career, Shastri played 80 Test matches, scoring 3,830 runs and taking 151 wickets. He also played 150 One Day Internationals, scoring 3,108 runs and taking 129 wickets.
